The Ceremony

What Is a Nikkah?

"And We created you in pairs." Qur'an 78:8

A nikkah is the Islamic marriage ceremony: a sacred covenant made before Allah, witnessed by loved ones, and rooted in consent, mercy, responsibility, and sincerity.

During the ceremony, the marriage contract is accepted, the mahr is acknowledged, and a short reminder may be shared. Guests are invited to listen respectfully, make du'a, and celebrate the beginning of a marriage with barakah.

Guest Guide

Q+A

Can I bring a guest?

This is an invite-only celebration, and seats are reserved per head. Please refer to the names listed on your invitation. If you have any questions, reach out to Mariam or Umar.

Are kids allowed?

We love your little ones, but this celebration will be adults only. Thank you for understanding and helping us keep seating comfortable for invited guests.

Is there a dress code?

Yes. Please come in formal and modest champagne-gold inspired attire. Abayas, native wear, dresses, suits, and elegant evening wear are all welcome. Suggested colors are champagne, gold, and ivory.

Will food be served?

Yes. Nigerian cuisine and small chops will be served after the nikkah ceremony, insha'Allah.

If I am not Muslim, do I need to cover my hair?

No, you do not need to cover your hair. We simply ask all guests to dress formally and modestly out of respect for the nikkah ceremony.

What should I expect during the nikkah?

The nikkah is a short Islamic marriage ceremony with a reminder, the marriage contract, and du'a. Guests are welcome to sit, listen, and share in the joy of the moment.

Should I arrive early?

Yes, please arrive by 2:30 pm so everyone can be seated before the 3:00 pm ceremony begins.
